The problem

CaveatSmall sample size. Not statistically significant.

Every design change followed the same path: I designed it, wrote a ticket, and waited. With a two-person tech team focused on model quality, design tickets were usually deprioritized.

The fixes already existed. The problem was getting them shipped.

Where this is going

When I started, I thought design was mostly about visual craft. Over the years I learned that most design problems are decision problems: people trying to agree on what to build and why.

AI changed the part in between. Turning a decision into working software got fast — I've spent the past year shipping my own design changes to production. What didn't get faster is the ends: understanding what's worth building, and checking whether it worked for a real person. That's where I put my time.
