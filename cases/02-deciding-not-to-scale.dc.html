TL;DR — We spent six months measuring whether Planificando was creating real value for teachers, instead of guessing. The evidence showed initial use without sustained adoption, and located the real drop-off in a different funnel step than the team assumed. The conclusion was to redesign the core flow rather than scale the current product. That redesign (V2) is now in prototype.

Context

By late 2025 Planificando had users but no clear picture of whether it was working. The team's stated goal for the semester: move from intuition-based validation to evidence-based validation. Measure, observe, document, learn.

What I built

  • The validation methodology and instruments: product-side and classroom-side, with an observation protocol for the pilot.
  • Usability tests and field work: workshops in schools, individual tests with teachers over 50, a usability round in Vicente López, session replays.
  • The UX report: 12 versions, crossing usage data (Aug 2025–Mar 2026), qualitative feedback, email campaigns, and analytics. Cleaned base: 189 real users, 105 active, 56% activation. 17 issues prioritized P0/P1.

What the data showed

The board attributed the activation drop to the step between clicking "create" and generating a plan. The funnel data showed that step converts at 95.6%. The real drop, around 50%, was one step earlier: between login and clicking "create". Teachers weren't failing to generate; they weren't starting.

Combined with the qualitative evidence, the picture was consistent: teachers used the product with guidance, but organic, sustained adoption wasn't happening with the current flow.

The decision

The product team's conclusion at the end of the semester was to redefine the core flow rather than scale the current version. My part in that decision was the evidence on the table: the UX report and the funnel analysis were two of the inputs the team weighed. The redesign became V2, a conversational, step-by-step generation flow. I built the first prototype (three entry points, five-step flow) in June 2026. What the redesign changed is case 04.

Caveats

  1. Small base: 189 users. Every number here is a directional signal, not statistical proof.
  2. Fine-grained metrics only exist from March 20, 2026, when Mixpanel was instrumented. Earlier conclusions rest on coarser data and qualitative work.
  3. V2 is a prototype. Whether the redesign fixes adoption is an open question; this case documents the decision, not its outcome.
